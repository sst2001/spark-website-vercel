Shlomo Touboul

Founder

Shlomo Touboul is a pioneering figure in the cybersecurity industry, whose entrepreneurial journey began in the early days of Israel's tech scene, a time when startups were rare and venture capital scarce. Against this challenging backdrop, he founded Shany, a venture that not only thrived but also caught the attention of Israel's first public VC, Mofet, and subsequently, the tech giant Intel. The acquisition of Shany by Intel marked a significant milestone, showcasing the potential of Israeli technology on the global stage.


Following his success with Shany, Shlomo's innovative drive led him to establish Finjan in 1996, a company that introduced behavior-based anti-malware technology, fundamentally changing the cybersecurity landscape. Finjan's groundbreaking approach propelled it to a Nasdaq listing and eventual acquisition, further cementing Shlomo's reputation as a visionary leader.


Beyond Finjan, Shlomo continued to impact the cybersecurity domain through the founding of Yoggie and AirEye and his role as CEO of Illusive Networks. With a career underscored by over 40 disruptive technologies and patents, Shlomo's work has not only achieved an Israeli record in patent revenue, generating over $400 million, but has also significantly advanced the field of cybersecurity. His story is a testament to the power of innovation, visionary leadership, and the transformative impact of entrepreneurial spirit in the tech industry.

Program Timetable Example

The program's schedule is tailored to accommodate the unique needs of each organization and industry. Typically, it begins with assembling a core team, often comprising members from Engineering, Research, and Product Management. This is followed by bi-weekly briefings, lasting 1-2 hours, to ensure all participants are well-prepared and aligned. The highlight of the program is an intensive IP offsite, spanning two to three days, designed to foster innovation and strategic thinking. Depending on the project's evolution, additional sessions or a one-day offsite may be organized to finalize patent applications, facilitated by our expert in-house patent editor, ensuring precision and professionalism in the submission process
